5.2 Gains from Trade

Gains from trade can be grouped into two categories: gains from exchange, and gains from specialization.

Drawing supplies from a world market allows access to a wider array of products at usually lower cost and perhaps with greater security of supply than can be produced by domestic industries. Lower prices from imported goods allow consumers to buy more goods from disposable income. Price benefits also for raw materials used to produce final goods, lowering price of these final goods. As incomes rise, savings will increase, which will lead to increased investment and greater economic growth.

Trade will stimulate investment in and expansion of low cost industries (and will force contraction of high cost industries). Increase of the size of the market allows firms or industry to take advantage of economies of scale. Increase of competition provides greater emphasis on technological development and innovation, and results in increased skills of workforce.
